Hydrogel Membranes for Thin-Film Boiling

As an undergraduate researcher in the Cai Group at UC San Diego, I worked on improving thin-film boiling performance using hydrogel-coated glass fiber membranes. I was responsible for developing a repeatable fabrication process that bonded hydrogel to the membrane without infiltrating the porous fibers — a key requirement for maintaining coating structure and performance. Through iterative trials, I developed a successful method using layered tape borders to control coating thickness, later refined with a doctor blade.

I also created the thickness-measurement approach for the project: imaging coated membranes under a microscope alongside a razor blade of known thickness and converting pixel distances into real dimensions, giving the group a reliable way to quantify coating uniformity and validate the fabrication process.

Rocket Project at UCLA — Prometheus

On the engine team, I work on the design and development of the engine nozzle through trade studies, CAD modeling, and system-level coordination with the rest of the propulsion assembly — balancing performance, manufacturability, and cost while ensuring proper fit and interface compatibility with the injector, chamber, and ignitor systems.

Previously, I helped design, fabricate, and test propulsion hardware including static fire test stands and bottle inverters, performed water-flow tests on engine injectors, and conducted hydrostatic tests on engine chambers to verify structural performance before higher-risk testing.

RC Aircraft — Design, Build, Fly

I led a four-person team through the full design-build-test cycle of an RC airplane built from scratch to meet mission requirements. Starting from a CAD-based design, we manufactured the aircraft using foam structures, 3D-printed components, and integrated electrical and propulsion systems. I used CAD and XFLR5 to analyze center of gravity and wing placement to ensure stable flight.

In final testing, our aircraft achieved a self-propelled takeoff, sustained a 2.5-minute flight, exceeded 80 ft/s in straight-line speed, performed two barrel rolls, and landed safely — the only team in the course to achieve both powered takeoff and sustained flight.
